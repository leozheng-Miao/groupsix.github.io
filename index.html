Cybersecurity

What does it to do?

Cybersecurity means that the software, hardware and the data contained in the network system are protected from damage, change, leakage and intrusion due to sudden or malicious reasons. The network system can operate normally and continuously, and the network service will not be interrupted. Cybersecurity refers to the organization and collection of resources, processes and structures, which are used to protect cyberspace and cyberspace supported systems from events inconsistent with the property rights of defectors (Craigen, Dan; Diakun-Thibault, Nadia; Purse, Randy, 2014)

In order to make the network can smoothly implement the function of resource sharing, it is necessary to ensure the normal operation of network hardware and software, as well as the security of data information interaction. For example, the personal information (such as bank account number and login password) transmitted on the network is not found by others, which is the requirement of users for the confidentiality of the information transmitted on the network. At present, cybersecurity is not only related to privacy and property security, but also directly related to personal safety. Looking forward to the new ecological construction of digital security, we need to understand the security problems from a new perspective, jump out of the traditional concept of attack and defense, promote the linkage of the government, enterprises and users on the basis of cooperation, and jointly enhance the awareness of protection to prevent the fracture of a link in the chain. In the era of digital economy, security is no longer the problem of a single enterprise or industry. To weave the "protection net" of cybersecurity and build a comprehensive security protection system requires the cooperation of various institutions and fields.

Due to the rapid development of computer technology, network information has become an important guarantee for the harmonious development of society. Cybersecurity is mainly to prevent man-made attacks from all over the world, such as computer viruses, data tampering, information leakage, theft and so on. At the same time, the network entity has to be tested by electromagnetic radiation, fire, earthquake and other natural factors. The Futurist Stephen Steele (2009) pointed out: "the network system... Is not just information, but network culture. Multi-level coordinated cyber-attacks will be able to carry out large-scale (national security system), medium-sized (local power grid) and small-scale (car launched) destruction at the same time. Eichensehr, Kristen E (2017) mentioned that the threat of cybersecurity (1) Destroy infected computer networks used by transnational criminal groups ("botnet cancellation"). (2) Fix software vulnerabilities that can be used for criminal, spying and offensive operations ("zero-day vulnerability"). (3) Attribute network intrusion to state sponsored attackers. (4) Protect private systems and networks from complex state sponsored attackers.

First of all, in the next few years, due to the further strengthening of the role of information warfare in military decision-making and operations, cybersecurity becomes an important means to protect state secrets and sensitive information. Second, Forbes insight 1 conducted a redesigned survey on information security in the era of digital transformation in 2017. According to the report, 69% of senior executives think that digital transformation makes them reconsider cybersecurity policy (Li, Ning; Ash, Ivan; Anwar, 2019). Which means in the next few years, information security managers and cybersecurity engineers need to develop a perfect information system security policy (ISSP) to protect the company's confidential information, assets, data and other important information.

With the wide use of Internet technology, radio frequency identification technology, wireless broadband technology and other new technologies, there is a new possibility: many information technologies can be used secretly, so that powerful hackers can repeatedly invade the opponent's computer network, illegally control, interfere with, interrupt the user's system, illegally collect, store, process and use relevant information. On the system of network information security, this is based on the communication protocol, operating system, database for information maintenance, control, detection. The core part is not allowed to be accessed or modified at will.

What is the likely impact?

The potential factors affecting the development of cybersecurity mainly include the following aspects: (1) network structure factors: LAN, intranet and extranet. In the construction of internal network, in order to achieve the requirements of network openness, it is often necessary to give up part of the security mechanism. （2) Network protocol factor: the general Internet companies have higher and higher requirements for the compatibility of network protocols, so that the protocols between companies can be interconnected, compatible and communicate. While bringing benefits to the company, it also brings certain security risks. （3) Regional factors.（4) User factor: with the increase of users, there may be some business spies or hackers. （5) Host factor: in order to make LAN and single machine interconnected, the types of host are increased, such as workstation and server. Their network system and operating system are very different, and the loopholes brought by the system may cause greater security risks.

As a result of the above factors may bring the following changes: (1) strengthen cybersecurity: comprehensive development of cybersecurity strategy, the use of firewalls, recording activities and so on. (2) Protection of application security: mainly for specific applications (such as web server, network payment software system) established by the security measures. （3) Protection of system security: it refers to the security protection from the perspective of the overall e-commerce system or network payment system. （4) New encryption technologies are produced: symmetric encryption and asymmetric encryption.

What does it do?

Robot technology, design, construct, and use robots to perform traditional tasks. Robots were originally used to deal with monotonous tasks (such as making cars on assembly lines), but later were far beyond their original use.Mechanical bots come in all shapes and sizes to efficiently carry out the task for which they are designed. All robots vary in design, functionality and degree of autonomy. From the 0.2 millimeter-long “RoboBee” to the 200 meter-long robotic shipping vessel “Vindskip,” robots are emerging to carry out tasks that humans simply can’t. Generally, there are five types of robots:

1. Pre-Programmed Robots

Pre-programmed robots operate in a controlled environment where they do simple, monotonous tasks.And its job is to perform that task longer, faster and more efficiently than a human.

2. Humanoid Robots

Humanoid robots are robots that look like and/or mimic human behavior. These robots usually perform human-like activities (like running, jumping and carrying objects), and are sometimes designed to look like us, even having human faces and expressions. Two of the most prominent examples of humanoid robots are Hanson Robotics’ Sophia (in the video above) and Boston Dynamics’ Atlas.

3. Autonomous Robots

Autonomous robots operate independently of human operators. These robots are usually designed to carry out tasks in open environments that do not require human supervision. They are quite unique because they use sensors to perceive the world around them, and then employ decision-making structures (usually a computer) to take the optimal next step based on their data and mission. An example of an autonomous robot would be the Roomba vacuum cleaner, which uses sensors to roam freely throughout a home.

4. Teleoperated Robots

Teleoperated robots are semi-autonomous bots that use a wireless network to enable human control from a safe distance. These robots usually work in extreme geographical conditions, weather, circumstances, etc. Examples of teleoperated robots are the human-controlled submarines used to fix underwater pipe leaks during the BP oil spill or drones used to detect landmines on a battlefield.

5. Augmenting Robots

Augmenting robots either enhance current human capabilities or replace the capabilities a human may have lost. The field of robotics for human augmentation is a field where science fiction could become reality very soon, with bots that have the ability to redefine the definition of humanity by making humans faster and stronger. Some examples of current augmenting robots are robotic prosthetic limbs or exoskeletons used to lift hefty weights.（Builtin，2021）
